This can lead to serious distraction. I can be working away and think "I need to eat something." Then my mind goes to work figuring out what to eat that is healthy, how to prep it, and wondering if I have the stuff to make it. Then off to the kitchen I go to fix it. After that there is the eating. Sometimes to save time I bring the food back to my desk thinking I will work while I eat but really I get very little done while I am eating. The whole process is pretty time-consuming. And since it is healthy it seems like I am doing a "good" thing even though my work productivity is suffering. My most effective way to manage this distraction is to plan ahead. I can plan the best times to stop and eat. That way I don't eat too often or forget to eat (which can make me less effective even if I keep working.)Then I can prep the food ahead of time before I start work. That way when it is time to eat my break from work is much shorter. Linda DuHadway (heading level 3)

Distraction:Roommates

Since I live with my significant other, my mom, and my stepdad, trying to focus on things I'm working on can be tricky. Despite sharing a decently sized house, It can feel very cramped in spaces shared by the four of us, often needing to push past when trying to do dishes or cook. Relaxing can be difficult because of all the noise other people make too. Trying to get anything done when at least one other person is in the house can prove troublesome.

In order to work around these hurdles, I try to schedule things so there's very little overelap. Cooking meals, taking care of chores, and using water all at different times to avoid fighting over resources. If needed I will wear headphones while working on my projects and schoolwork to drowned out background noise.
