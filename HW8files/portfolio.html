The Axolotl

This adorable water creature is a Paedomorphic Salamander. Its native name is pronounced "Ah-Sho-Lut", but for the many people who are not familiar with Classical Nahuatl, the name is pronounced "Axe-Oh-Lot-L". Adults range in length from 15cm to 45cm and weight between 2oz and 8oz. These creatures are unique not only for their looks, but also their biology. Those feather-like protrusions around their head are not just for show, they are actually external gills that allow the Axolotl to breathe underwater like a fish!
